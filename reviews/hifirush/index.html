Hi Fi Rush

Score: 10/10

Playtime: 12 hours









A rhythm-based action game that’s as cheesy as it is endearing. The combat, the writing and the pacing were all hits.

Story. The writing’s fun, bringing a lot of humour and levity in a cheesy, tongue-in-cheek kind of way. It never overstays its welcome and blends perfectly with the game’s look and feel.

Graphics. Feel the beat! Everything in the game, from the environment to the enemies to your character’s animations whether he’s idling, moving or fighting, follows the beat of the level’s music. The whole game tries to support you in staying on the beat. The cartoony style the devs chose is cleanly realized and works well with the game’s tone. Occasionally during boss fights and cutscenes, they’ll bring out alternate art styles for single frames, drawing inspiration from Borderlands for “boss intro cards” and Jojo’s for silly poses and over-the-top shading. All of it works together to deliver an experience that’s a feast for the eyes.

Combat. Combos! Moves galore! While the combat might stand up to combo brawler giants Bayonetta and Devil May Cry, it certainly doesn’t slack. There’s nothing I can fault it for, and the rhythm system makes it stand on its own, able to provide a fun experience different from its counterparts. My biggest plus is that the game is newbie friendly, and you can beat it while being terrible at keeping to the beat, although your scores might be abysmal because of it. S-rank score, S-rank clear time, C-rank Just Timing :yea:

For those that aren’t musically crippled, there’s a challenge mode and mission achievements to aim for that look like a good time!

Overall, Hi Fi Rush is a success on all fronts. I loved the characters, the story, the gameplay, the graphics and the game’s pacing and length were perfect.
