Torchlight 2

Score: 5/10

Playtime: 22 hours (DNF)









Torchlight 2 is an isometric ARPG released in 2012 by Runic Games.

Gameplay. Like any ARPG, your time is a 60-20-20 split between mowing down enemies, planning your build, and managing your inventory (except Path of Exile, that one's a 20-60-20 split). On the positive side, Torchlight 2 has all the hallmarks of a successful experience: varied skill trees, distinctive classes (including an Engineer with a big hammer!), buckets of items and a fun, goofy art style. Despite that, I didn't finish the game and I can identify two reasons why.

First, the difficulty in the game is poorly implemented. I tried Normal, Veteran and Elite, and what I found was that Normal was so easy I could tape my mouse down and let the game play itself like a more boring Cookie Clicker. Veteran was like Normal but slower due to inflated health bars, and Elite was so slow it felt like playing WoW Classic. I ended up installing a 3x XP mod to hopefully have a good gameplay experience on Elite, and what I found is that the leveling speed felt normal at 3x speed, but the enemies were still horrible damage sponges. Not even the thrill of hardcore could save the experience, as the game balance also suffers from random difficulty spikes: casually stroll through hordes of goblins, then in the same zone encounter a group of yeti mages for the first time and watch as the death screen appears faster than you can blink. Note that this is on Elite; on Normal my eyebrow would raise a millimeter as the yetis were the only thing to make my health bar move perceptibly in the last half hour.

Second, and this might be intrinsically tied to the first, but progressing through your build, unlocking new skills and gaining in power didn't feel that exciting. Part of this, I think, is that the base skills you start with are more than enough to beat the whole game with (yes, even on Elite - in fact the recommended build on Elite is an embermage with the starting pew pew spell and nothing else other than buff passives). It lacks the power spikes and synergies of other ARPGs, at least during the first half of the game which is a significant amount of time. This isn't helped by a poor respec system that locks you in to what you've picked (unless you mod it).

Story. Serviceable in the barest way that ARPG stories often are, I didn't feel any pull towards the NPCs or stories being presented. They existed as a vehicle to push you towards new zones and enemies.

Art. My favourite part of the game; the exaggerated cartoony art style reminds me of Warcraft. The SFX are also very satisfying.

Torchlight 2, for me, failed to deliver the core enjoyment of an arpg, that is, planning and executing a build and feeling the power spikes as you mow down hordes of mobs, and so failed to hold my attention. It's the first ARPG to do so, and so I can't recommend it, although I could see it being a fun coop experience.
